01 — Mood Disorder Questionnaire
The MDQ screens for bipolar spectrum disorders. It asks about experiences you may have had at any point in your life — not just recently. Answer based on your lifetime experience.

02 — Patient Health Questionnaire
The PHQ-9 measures the severity of depressive symptoms over the past two weeks. Unlike the MDQ which looks at lifetime experiences, the PHQ-9 focuses on how you have been feeling recently.
How to respond

Over the last 2 weeks, how often have you been bothered by each of the following?

P01 — Little interest or pleasure in doing things ✓
P02 — Feeling down, depressed, or hopeless ✓
P03 — Trouble falling or staying asleep, or sleeping too much ✓
P04 — Feeling tired or having little energy ✓
P05 — Poor appetite or overeating ✓
P06 — Feeling bad about yourself — or that you are a failure or have let yourself or your family down ✓
P07 — Trouble concentrating on things, such as reading the newspaper or watching television ✓
P08 — Moving or speaking so slowly that other people could have noticed — or the opposite, being so fidgety or restless that you have been moving around much more than usual ✓
P09 — Thoughts that you would be better off dead, or thoughts of hurting yourself in some way ✓
